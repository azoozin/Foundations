Feijoada

Feijoada is a common name given to dishes from Portuguese-speaking countries such as Portugal, Brazil, Angola, East Timor, Mozambique, and Macau,
where it is made from a mixture of meat and white, black, or red beans, usually accompanied by rice.

Ingredients

  • Black beans
  • Sausage (Calabresa)
  • Pig meat (including ears, etc.)
  • Salted dry beef

  • Seasonings and aromatics:
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Bay leaves
  • Tomato
  • Salt and pepper.

How to make Feijoada

  1. The night before:Your preparation for feijoada must start the night before, by soaking the beans. (This is very important to achieve the proper texture in the stew.)
  2. Day of:
    Cook or brown the meat, in batches and set aside.
    Start the aromatics sauteing, then add the soaked beans, meat, water, and other ingredients.
    Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook until the beans are soft.
